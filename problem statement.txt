01. PROBLEM IDENTIFICATION
================================================================

Every year, thousands of Indians die not from their medical
emergency — but from the gap between when it happens and when
professional help arrives.

India has over 600,000 villages. The average ambulance response
time in rural areas is 30–60 minutes. In that window, the people
present — a family member, a neighbor, a passerby — are the only
line of defence. They have no training, no guidance, and often
panic or take the wrong action.

Wrong first-aid kills:
- Tilting the head of a seizure patient can block the airway
- Applying toothpaste or oil on a burn worsens tissue damage
- Moving a person with a spinal injury can cause paralysis
- Improper CPR technique is ineffective and can fracture ribs

And when internet IS available — no single tool exists that
combines real-time AI diagnosis, live ambulance tracking,
telemedicine, medicine identification, and multilingual
support in one place for the Indian rural user.

What problem are we solving?
Preventable deaths and health deterioration caused by incorrect,
absent, or inaccessible medical guidance — both in emergencies
and in everyday health management — across rural and semi-urban India.

Who is facing this problem?
Rural and semi-urban families across India — over 800 million
people — who lack access to trained medical help within the
first critical minutes of an emergency, and who have no
reliable tool for everyday health decisions either.

Why is this problem important?
India records over 1.5 lakh accidental deaths annually
(NCRB 2022). A significant portion occur in the critical
window before ambulance arrival. Most are preventable with
correct first-aid. Beyond emergencies, 65% of India's
population has no access to a doctor within 5km.

02. TARGET USER
================================================================

Primary User (Emergency Mode — Offline):
Adult family member or bystander (age 20-55) in a rural or
semi-urban Indian household. Basic Android smartphone user.
Speaks Hindi, Kannada, Tamil, Telugu, Marathi, or Bengali.
Has no medical training. May have no internet at the moment
of crisis.

Secondary User (Smart Mode — Online):
Semi-urban or urban Indian with intermittent internet —
uses JeevanAid for everyday health queries, family health
records, medicine identification, and doctor consultations.

Challenges They Face:
- No first-aid training or access to any training
- Language barrier — most health tools are English-only
- No internet in emergency moments (rural connectivity gaps)
- Panic — no structured guidance on what to do first
- Everyday health decisions made without doctor access
- No way to identify medicines or track family health history

================================================================
03. PAIN POINTS
================================================================

Biggest frustration:
"I didn't know what to do and I just watched." — the
helplessness of untrained bystanders in a crisis.

What is currently not working:
- 108 connects but operator cannot guide complex procedures
- YouTube requires internet + search + correct language match
- Existing first-aid apps are English-only and text-heavy
- No single app combines emergency + everyday health needs
- Telemedicine apps are too complex for rural users
- No medicine identifier works in Indian regional languages

Why existing solutions are failing:
- Designed for urban, educated, English-speaking users
- Require stable internet — useless in actual emergencies
- Fragmented — need 5 different apps for 5 different needs
- No audio guidance for low-literacy users

================================================================
04. DESIRED OUTCOME (GAINS)
================================================================

Offline: User follows guided first-aid steps and keeps the
patient stable until the ambulance arrives. Family feels
equipped and confident — not helpless.

Online: User gets AI-powered diagnosis, connects with a real
doctor, tracks the ambulance live, identifies medicines, and
manages family health records — all in one app, in their language.

What does success look like?
"I knew exactly what to do. I could see the ambulance coming.
The doctor told me what to do next. The patient survived."

================================================================
05. CURRENT SCENARIO
================================================================

How is this problem solved today?
- Call 108 and wait — no active guidance during the wait
- Call a relative who may or may not know what to do
- Search YouTube (needs internet, time, and luck)
- Use fragmented apps — maps here, doctors elsewhere,
  no actual first-aid in regional languages anywhere
- Most common outcome: do nothing and panic

Limitations of current tools:
- All assume internet availability
- None localized, visual, or designed for panic
- No structured step-by-step guidance in regional languages
- Everyday health tools are too complex or English-only
- No single integrated solution for rural India exists

06. YOUR IDEA — JeevanAid (Hybrid PWA)
================================================================

JeevanAid is a Hybrid Progressive Web App. It works completely
offline as a full emergency first-aid guide. When internet is
available, it unlocks a powerful AI-driven health platform.
One install via browser link. No app store needed.

------------------------------------------------------------
OFFLINE MODE — Always Available Core
------------------------------------------------------------

1. EMERGENCY FIRST-AID GUIDE
   Large icon-based emergency selector for 12 emergencies:
   Snake Bite | Seizure | Choking | Burns | Heart Attack |
   Drowning | Fracture | Severe Bleeding | Unconsciousness |
   Allergic Reaction | Electric Shock | Heat Stroke

   Per emergency: step-by-step illustrated guide, audio
   narration in regional language, looping video clips for
   key procedures, bold DO NOT warning boxes for myths.

2. LOCAL LANGUAGE SUPPORT (OFFLINE)
   Hindi | Kannada | Tamil | Telugu | Marathi | Bengali
   All content pre-cached. Zero internet needed.

3. ONE-TAP 108 EMERGENCY CALL
   Persistent sticky button on every single screen.

4. NEAREST CLINIC FINDER (OFFLINE)
   Cached OpenStreetMap tiles + pre-loaded PHC/hospital
   database. Nearest 3 facilities with distance + call.

5. BASIC SYMPTOM TRIAGE (OFFLINE)
   Yes/No question flow → urgency categorization →
   routes to correct first-aid module or 108 call.

6. CAREGIVER DAILY GUIDE (OFFLINE)
   Home management for diabetes, hypertension, TB, cardiac
   patients. Medication reminders + visual pill identifiers.

7. ASHA WORKER MODE (OFFLINE)
   Patient visit logging + immunization tracking.
   Replaces paper registers entirely.

------------------------------------------------------------
ONLINE MODE — AI-Powered Smart Features
------------------------------------------------------------

8. AI SYMPTOM DIAGNOSIS
   Describe symptoms in any Indian language → AI returns
   likely condition + urgency level + immediate action.
   Clean card UI with color-coded urgency (red/amber/green).

9. LIVE 108 AMBULANCE TRACKER
   After calling 108 — real-time ambulance location on map
   + live ETA countdown. Animated pulse indicator on map.
   "Help is 8 minutes away." Eliminates panic of not knowing.

10. TELEMEDICINE — DOCTOR ON CALL
    One-tap video or chat consult with verified MBBS doctor.
    Clean booking card: symptom input + language preference.
    Live queue position shown. Callback option available.

11. AI MEDICINE IDENTIFIER
    Photo of any pill or packaging → AI instantly identifies
    medicine name, dosage, purpose, side effects in regional
    language. Critical for caregivers managing elderly patients.

12. LIVE HOSPITAL BED AVAILABILITY
    Nearest hospitals with real-time emergency bed status.
    Color coded — Green (available) / Red (full).
    Integrates with NHA Ayushman Bharat state health APIs.

13. MULTILINGUAL AI HEALTH CHATBOT
    Full conversational AI for any health question in any
    Indian language. Persistent chat history. Escalates
    to 108 automatically if life-threatening situation
    detected in conversation.

14. FAMILY HEALTH RECORDS VAULT
    Blood group, allergies, conditions, prescriptions per
    family member. One-tap PDF share with emergency doctors.
    Syncs to secure cloud when online.

15. COMMUNITY DISEASE ALERT SYSTEM
    Detects local outbreak patterns from crowdsourced data
    + government feeds. Push notification to nearby users
    with prevention steps in local language.

16. POST-EMERGENCY FOLLOW-UP
    48-hour recovery guide after any emergency: medication
    reminders, warning signs, follow-up clinic locator,
    daily check-in prompts.

07. BASIC WORKING
================================================================

OFFLINE FLOW:
1. User opens JeevanAid link in browser → installs as PWA
2. Selects language on first launch (saved permanently)
3. All first-aid content, maps, audio, video cached locally
4. Emergency → tap type → visual + audio guide instantly
5. 108 button persistent on every screen
6. Nearest clinic shown from cached offline map

ONLINE FLOW:
1. App detects internet → Online Mode badge activates
2. Home screen upgrades with AI Dashboard cards:
   AI Diagnosis | Doctor Call | Medicine Scan |
   Live Ambulance | Hospital Beds | Health Vault
3. All online features accessible in 1-2 taps from home
4. Family health vault syncs to encrypted cloud
5. AI chatbot available for any health conversation

Technology Stack (high-level):
- Frontend: HTML, CSS, JavaScript PWA + Service Worker
- Offline storage: Cache API + IndexedDB
- AI features: LLM API for diagnosis + multilingual chat
- Maps: Leaflet.js (offline tiles + online live tracking)
- Medicine ID: Computer Vision API (online only)
- Telemedicine: WebRTC for video consult
- Backend: Node.js + Firebase for online features
- Kiro: Mandatory development environment (Track 1)

================================================================
09. CHALLENGES
================================================================

Building challenges:
- Medical accuracy: Steps must be validated by certified doctor
- True offline AI not feasible: mitigated by rule-based offline
  triage + cloud AI for online mode
- Audio/video content in 6+ languages (post-hackathon effort)
- UX must be panic-proof and icon-first for low-literacy users

Risks:
- Liability if incorrect guidance causes harm
  (mitigation: doctor validation, clear disclaimers,
  certified health organization partnerships)
- API costs for AI features at scale
  (mitigation: freemium + government/NGO partnerships)
- Offline map accuracy depends on cache recency
